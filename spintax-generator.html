Frequently Asked Questions

What is spintax?

Spintax (short for "spin syntax") is a format that allows you to create multiple variations of text by using a specific structure. In cold emails, it helps you create unique variations of your message for each recipient, making your outreach feel more personalized and less like mass spam.

How does spintax work?

Spintax uses curly brackets {} to enclose variations of words or phrases, separated by vertical bars |. For example, the spintax {Hello|Hi|Hey} would randomly choose one of "Hello," "Hi," or "Hey" each time the message is generated. Our tool automatically identifies opportunities for variation and creates the proper syntax for you.

Will using spintax improve my email deliverability?

Yes! Email providers and spam filters are increasingly flagging identical messages sent in bulk. By using spintax, each recipient gets a unique version of your email, significantly reducing the chances of being caught by spam algorithms. Our users typically see a 15-30% improvement in inbox placement.

How many variations can I generate with spintax?

The number of variations multiplies with each spintax element you include. For example, if you have 3 different options in five different places, you could generate up to 3^5 = 243 unique variations of your email. Our tool shows you the total possible combinations after generating your spintax.

Can I use spintax with my email marketing software?

Most modern cold email platforms like Lemlist, Mailshake, and Reply.io support spintax natively. For other platforms like Gmail or Outlook, you can use dedicated extensions that process spintax before sending. Check your email tool's documentation for specific syntax requirements—our generator creates standard spintax that works with most platforms.

Best Practices for Using Spintax in Cold Emails

  1. Focus on high-impact areas: Subject lines, greetings, and calls-to-action benefit most from variation.

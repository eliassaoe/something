How to Identify Legitimate AI Tools in 2023

With the proliferation of AI tools and services, learning to distinguish legitimate offerings from potentially fake ones like JustDone AI is essential. Here are key verification strategies for evaluating any AI tool before investing time or money.

Verify the Company Behind the AI Tool

Legitimate AI companies typically have verifiable business information and a digital footprint extending beyond their own website:

  • Company registration: Verifiable business registration in the jurisdiction they claim to operate
  • Team information: Real people with LinkedIn profiles and verifiable backgrounds
  • Funding information: For startups, information about investors or funding rounds
  • Physical address: A real office location, not just a P.O. box
  • Digital footprint: Mentions in industry publications, conference presentations, etc.

For B2B tools specifically, having access to quality contact information is crucial. Services like email extraction tools can be valuable for verifying business legitimacy when properly used.

Check Independent Reviews and User Testimonials

Look beyond the testimonials on the company's own website, which can easily be fabricated. Reliable sources include:

  • Professional review sites like G2, Capterra, or TrustPilot
  • Industry forums and discussion boards
  • Social media searches for organic mentions
  • YouTube reviews from established technology reviewers
  • Case studies with verifiable companies

"The difference between legitimate AI tools and fake ones becomes obvious when you look at independent user experiences. Real AI products have a mix of positive and constructive feedback with specific details about functionality."

Test Trial Versions Before Committing

Most legitimate AI tools offer some form of free trial or demonstration:

  • Time-limited free trials (7-14 days is common)
  • Feature-limited free tiers
  • Interactive demos on their website
  • Money-back guarantee periods

During your trial, test the tool with varied inputs to verify it produces unique, contextually appropriate outputs. This testing phase is crucial for determining if the claims of capabilities align with the actual performance.

Protecting Yourself from Fraudulent AI Services

With growing evidence suggesting JustDone AI is fake, it's important to understand how to protect yourself from similar questionable services in the future. Here are practical strategies for due diligence and recourse if you've already been affected.

Due Diligence Before Investing in AI Services

Before committing to any AI service, particularly those making extraordinary claims, follow these protective steps:

  • Start small: Begin with the minimum commitment possible
  • Check payment protection: Use payment methods with good buyer protection like credit cards
  • Document everything: Save all marketing materials, emails, and claimed capabilities
  • Read the fine print: Pay special attention to refund policies and service guarantees
  • Cross-reference claimed clients: Try to verify if listed clients actually use the service

What to Do If You've Already Been Affected

If you suspect you've fallen victim to JustDone AI or a similar questionable service, take these steps:

  1. Document everything: Gather all communications, screenshots, and payment records
  2. Request a refund: Formally request a refund citing specific ways the service failed to deliver on promises
  3. Contact your payment provider: If the refund is denied, dispute the charge with your credit card company or payment service
  4. Report to authorities: File complaints with relevant consumer protection agencies:
    • Federal Trade Commission (FTC) in the US
    • The equivalent consumer protection agency in your country
    • Better Business Bureau
  5. Share your experience: Warn others by posting detailed, factual reviews on relevant platforms

"The best protection against fraudulent AI services is a combination of thorough research before purchasing and prompt action if you discover issues after the fact. Most legitimate companies value their reputation and will work to resolve problems."

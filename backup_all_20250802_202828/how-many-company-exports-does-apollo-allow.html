Understanding Apollo's Export Limits

Apollo.io structures its export limits based on subscription plans, with higher-tier plans offering more generous export allowances. Before diving into the specific numbers, it's important to understand that Apollo differentiates between company exports and contact exports, each with separate limitations.

Basic Plan Export Limits

The Basic plan is Apollo's entry-level offering, designed for individual users or small teams just getting started with prospecting:

  • Company Exports: 1,000 companies per month
  • Contact Exports: 2,500 contacts per month
  • Daily Export Limit: Approximately 250 exports per day (to prevent bulk exports)

This plan works well for smaller businesses with focused targeting needs or for testing the platform before upgrading.

Professional Plan Export Limits

The Professional plan is Apollo's most popular offering for growing sales teams:

  • Company Exports: 10,000 companies per month
  • Contact Exports: 15,000 contacts per month
  • Daily Export Limit: Approximately 1,000 exports per day

This tier represents a significant jump in export capacity, making it suitable for established sales operations with regular prospecting needs. Many users find the best balance of cost and export allowance with the Professional plan, though limits can still be restrictive for high-volume sales organizations.

Organization Plan Export Limits

For enterprise users with extensive prospecting requirements, the Organization plan offers the highest standard export limits:

  • Company Exports: Starting at 25,000 companies per month (negotiable based on contract)
  • Contact Exports: Starting at 30,000 contacts per month (also negotiable)
  • Daily Export Limit: Higher daily limits with more flexibility

Enterprise customers can sometimes negotiate custom export limits based on their specific needs and contract value. However, even at this level, there are still finite limitations to consider.

While Apollo offers significant export capabilities, all plans have definite limits that can impact your sales strategy. Understanding these boundaries is essential for planning your prospecting activities effectively.

Step-by-Step Guide to Export Companies from Apollo

Understanding how many company exports Apollo allows is only part of the equation. Knowing the proper export process helps ensure you don't waste your valuable export allowance. Here's a comprehensive guide to exporting companies efficiently:

Preparing Your List for Export

Before initiating any export, take these preparatory steps:

  1. Create and refine a targeted search using Apollo's filtering capabilities
  2. Review the preliminary results to ensure they align with your target criteria
  3. Sort your results by relevance or other priority factors
  4. Select the specific records you wish to export (rather than exporting entire pages)

This preparation phase is critical for optimizing your limited exports and ensuring you're capturing only the most valuable prospects.

The Export Process Explained

Once your list is prepared, follow these steps to export efficiently:

  1. Select Companies: Check the boxes next to companies you want to export, or select all on the current page
  2. Click Export: Use the "Export" button in the top right of the results view
  3. Choose Format: Select your preferred export format (CSV is most common)
  4. Select Fields: Choose which data fields to include in your export
  5. Confirm Export: Review usage of your export limit and confirm
  6. Download: Once processing is complete, download your export file

The system will display how many exports will be used from your monthly allowance before you confirm, giving you a chance to reconsider if needed.

Export Limitations and Compliance Requirements

Beyond the numerical limits of how many company exports Apollo allows, there are additional restrictions and compliance considerations that affect your export activities.

Time Restrictions Between Exports

Apollo implements several time-based restrictions to prevent automated bulk exports:

  • A cooling period may be enforced between large exports (typically 15-30 minutes)
  • Daily export limits prevent exhausting your monthly allowance too quickly
  • Automated or scripted exports are against Apollo's terms of service

These time restrictions are designed to ensure fair usage of the platform and maintain data quality standards. They also help prevent potential abuse of the export functionality.

Data Usage Policies

When exporting company data from Apollo, you must adhere to their data usage policies:

  • Exported data can only be used for legitimate business purposes
  • Reselling or redistributing exported data is prohibited
  • You must comply with applicable data protection laws (GDPR, CCPA, etc.)
  • Data retention should align with your privacy policy and legal requirements

Violating these policies can result in account restrictions or termination. Always ensure your usage of exported data complies with both Apollo's terms and relevant regulations.

Remember: Apollo's export limits aren't just about quantity—they're also governed by fair usage policies and compliance requirements that protect both the platform and the data subjects.

Frequently Asked Questions About Apollo Exports

Do company and contact exports count separately in Apollo?

Yes, Apollo maintains separate export allowances for companies and contacts. This means you can export up to your plan's limit for companies and still have your full contact export allowance available. For example, on the Professional plan, you can export 10,000 companies and 15,000 contacts each month as separate allocations.

What happens if I reach my Apollo export limit?

When you reach your monthly export limit, you'll be unable to export additional companies or contacts until your allowance refreshes the following month. Apollo displays your remaining export allowance in the export dialog, so you can monitor your usage. If you consistently reach your limits, consider upgrading your plan or exploring alternative sources for unlimited B2B leads.

Can I roll over unused Apollo exports to the next month?

No, Apollo's export allowances reset each month and do not roll over. Any unused exports from your monthly allocation are lost when the new month begins. This is why it's important to plan your export strategy to make the most of your available allowance throughout the month rather than saving them for later periods.

Is there a way to increase my Apollo export limit without upgrading plans?

Apollo occasionally offers temporary export limit increases as part of promotions or for specific use cases. You can contact their sales team to inquire about possible exceptions or temporary boosts. However, for consistently higher export needs, a plan upgrade is typically necessary. When Apollo's limits become a persistent constraint, many organizations supplement with alternative lead sources that offer more flexible export capabilities.

Does viewing company details in Apollo count toward my export limit?

No, simply viewing company details within the Apollo platform does not count toward your export limit. You're only charged against your export allowance when you actually download data or send it to an integrated system like your CRM. This allows you to freely browse and research companies without worrying about depleting your export allocation.
